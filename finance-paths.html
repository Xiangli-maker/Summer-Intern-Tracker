Princeton CS & ORFE — Where you fit
  • ORFE → Quant Finance is the most natural path. Stochastic calculus, probability, and optimization map directly to QF skills. Jane Street, D.E. Shaw, and Two Sigma actively recruit ORFE students.
  • CS → Quant Engineering — If you pair strong CS with some ORFE electives (ORF 245, ORF 309), you become a top candidate for quant trader and quant researcher roles.
  • CS → IB Tech — Goldman, JPMorgan, and Morgan Stanley all have dedicated technology analyst programs for CS students. Lower math bar, high hours, strong exit opps to PE/VC.
  • VC is the hardest to break into as an undergrad — most VCs prefer post-startup or post-IB analysts. Focus on QF or IB first, then lateral.
  • Apply early — IB and QF recruiting starts in August/September of your sophomore year for summer 2027 internships. Freshman year is for building skills and networking.
